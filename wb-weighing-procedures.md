# Weighing Procedures

## Overview

This procedure covers weighing N657CZ to establish empty weight and empty weight center of gravity (CG). Accurate leveling in both pitch and roll is critical — errors in aircraft attitude directly produce errors in the CG result.

For CG calculations from the recorded weights and moment arms, see [[W&B Calculation|wb-calculation]] in Section 4.

## Equipment Required

- Three platform scales, one per landing gear contact point (nose gear and two mains)
  - @@TOM: Source scales (EAA chapter loan or equivalent). Required accuracy: 0.1% or better, giving resolution of 0.5–1 lb on a 500–1000 lb load.
- Wooden drive-up ramps (one per scale) to bring scale platform flush with the hangar floor
  - @@TOM: Ramps not yet built. Design to bring scale surface to floor level so aircraft can be taxied onto scales without jacking.
- Plumb bob (one per weighing point)
- Chalk line
- Tape measure
- Digital or bubble level
- Tire pressure gauge and pump (for leveling adjustment)
- Chocks

## Safety Notes

> **NOTE:** Do not set the brakes while taking scale readings. Brake drag will affect scale accuracy. (AC 43.13, ch10_p015)

## Prerequisites

- Aircraft clean and free of excessive dirt, grease, and moisture (AC 43.13, ch10_p015)
- All equipment included in the empty weight configuration installed in the aircraft (AC 43.13, ch10_p015)
- Scales calibrated and zeroed per the scale manufacturer's instructions (AC 43.13, ch10_p015)
- Hangar doors closed — wind causes errors in scale readings (AC 43.13, ch10_p015)

## Aircraft Configuration

1. Drain the fuel system until the quantity indicators read zero with the aircraft in level flight attitude. Residual fuel remaining in tanks, lines, and engine is included in empty weight. (AC 43.13, ch10_p015)
2. Fill the oil system to the quantity specified in the aircraft records.
3. Retract all antennas, close all access panels, and stow all equipment in its normal installed position.

## Procedure

### 1. Position Scales and Ramps

1. Position the three scales on the hangar floor — one under each main gear location and one under the nose gear location.
2. Place drive-up ramps at the aft end of each scale so the aircraft can be taxied forward onto all three scales simultaneously.
   - @@TOM: Confirm ramp geometry provides a smooth transition without lifting any gear off the scales during rollup.
3. Chock the aircraft on the scales with all three gear on scale platforms.

### 2. Level the Aircraft

Level the aircraft in both pitch and roll before recording any readings. Do not use nose gear extension to adjust pitch — nose gear position affects the nose axle moment arm and will introduce CG error. Use tire pressure or blocks under the main gear tires.

1. Place a level fore and aft along the top of a longeron.
2. Adjust main gear tire pressure or place blocks under the appropriate tires to achieve level pitch attitude. Do not move the nose gear.
3. Place a level across both longerons just aft of the front seat bulkhead to check roll level.
4. Adjust tire pressure or blocking as needed until both pitch and roll levels read zero.
5. Recheck pitch level after any roll adjustment.

### 3. Record Scale Readings

1. With the aircraft level and brakes off, record the weight shown on each scale:
   - Left main: \_\_\_\_\_ lb
   - Right main: \_\_\_\_\_ lb
   - Nose gear: \_\_\_\_\_ lb
2. Verify total equals the expected approximate aircraft weight. If not, recheck level and re-record.
3. Note any tare weight (chocks, ramps contacting scale platforms) and deduct from readings. (AC 43.13, ch10_p015)

### 4. Locate Weighing Points

The weighing points are the axle centerlines of each landing gear, projected vertically to the floor. These must be measured to determine moment arms for the CG calculation.

1. Drop a plumb bob from the center of each axle to the hangar floor. Mark each floor position with chalk.
2. Snap a chalk centerline along the aircraft longitudinal axis.
3. From the nose gear chalk mark, drop a perpendicular to the centerline; mark the intersection. Repeat for each main gear mark.
4. Identify the wing leading edge station: drop a plumb bob from the wing leading edge and mark the floor.
   - @@TOM: Verify wing leading edge fuselage station is FS 113.9 for N657CZ.
5. Set a tape measure so that 113.9 in. aligns with the wing leading edge floor mark. All other station measurements are read directly from this tape.
6. Record the fuselage station of each weighing point (nose gear axle, left main axle, right main axle).

### 5. Document Results

Record the following in the aircraft weight and balance record:
- Date and location of weighing
- Scale make, model, and calibration date
- Aircraft fuel and oil configuration at time of weighing
- Weight at each gear position (with tare noted)
- Fuselage station of each weighing point
- Total empty weight
- Any equipment installed or missing relative to the standard empty weight configuration
