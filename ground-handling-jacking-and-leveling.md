# Jacking and Leveling

## Overview

This procedure covers lifting the main gear off the ground for maintenance and leveling the aircraft for weight and balance or rigging work. Two jacking methods are described; use the method appropriate to the available equipment.

The Cozy Mark IV has no factory-specified jack points. The accepted method is to support the aircraft under the center section spar or wing spar using appropriate pads.

## Equipment Required

**Method 1 — Nose Gear Extension:**
- Two padded sawhorses, height approximately 4 in. below the bottom of the center section spar with the nose gear retracted
- 4 in. thick, 2 lb/cu. ft. styrofoam pads (one per sawhorse)

**Method 2 — Floor Jacks:**
- Two floor jacks with sufficient rated capacity
- 12 in. x 12 in. x 3/4 in. plywood pads (one per jack)

**Both methods:**
- Digital or bubble level
- Assistant

## Safety Notes

> **NOTE:** Never jack directly on the wing or strake skins. Always support under the spar with a suitable pad. Concentrated loads on skins can cause structural damage.

Jack placement further outboard on the spar transfers more load to the nose gear. Preferred placement is at the outboard end of the center section spar or just outboard of the outboard wing mount bolts.

Perform all jacking in a closed hangar. Wind can affect stability of an aircraft on jacks.

## Prerequisites

- Aircraft fuel load recorded if jacking for weight and balance
- Area clear of obstructions beneath the wings and fuselage

## Procedure

### Method 1: Nose Gear Extension (Sawhorse)

1. Retract the nose gear so the nose rests on the ground.
2. Position one sawhorse under each end of the center section spar. Sawhorses should stand approximately 4 in. below the bottom of the spar with the nose gear retracted.
3. Place one styrofoam pad on top of each sawhorse, centered under the spar.
4. Confirm pads are properly positioned under the spar — not on the skins.
5. Have an assistant stand by to monitor spar contact and aircraft stability.
6. Extend the nose gear. The aircraft will rotate about the spar support points, lifting the main gear off the ground.
7. Verify both main gear are clear of the ground and the aircraft is stable on the sawhorses.

### Method 2: Floor Jacks

1. Position one floor jack with plywood pad under each end of the center section spar, or just outboard of the outboard wing mount bolts on the wing spar.
2. Confirm pads are positioned under the spar — not on the skins.
3. Have an assistant monitor spar contact and aircraft stability throughout.
4. Raise both jacks simultaneously in small increments, alternating sides to keep the load balanced.
5. Verify both main gear are clear of the ground and the aircraft is stable.

## Leveling

Perform leveling with the aircraft on jacks or with main gear on flat, level ground.

The leveling reference is the top surface of the longerons.

1. Place a level along the top of a longeron, oriented fore and aft.
2. Adjust nose gear extension (Method 1) or jack height (Method 2) until the level reads zero.
3. Place a level across both longerons just aft of the front seat bulkhead to check lateral level.
4. Adjust jack heights as needed until the lateral level reads zero.
5. Recheck fore-and-aft level after any lateral adjustment.

## Post-Procedure Checks

Whenever the aircraft is on jacks, check gear struts and wheels for abnormal play and correct as needed. (AC 43.13, ch09_p004)

Before lowering the aircraft, verify:
- All tools and equipment are clear of the landing gear path
- Nose gear is in the correct position for the intended lowering method
- Assistant is positioned to observe gear contact
