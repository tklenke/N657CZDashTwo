# Fuel Level Sensor Installation

## Overview

This procedure covers installation of a Stewart Warner 385B fuel level sender into a fuel tank. Perform this procedure when installing a new sender or reinstalling a sender after tank access.

## Tools and Materials Required

- Stewart Warner 385B sender (SWW-385B)
- Mounting bolts and washers (5 each) — verify length before installation; see step 3
- Neoprene mounting gasket (included with sender)
- Permatex Form-A-Gasket No. 2 Sealant
- @@TOM: verify connector pin count and wire colors at aircraft
- Isopropyl alcohol and clean cloths (surface prep)

## Safety Notes

**Bolt length is critical.** The mounting bolts must not protrude through the SAE-5 flange into the tank interior. If bolts penetrate the flange, they will contact the fiberglass skin and begin separating the flange and skin from the foam. Use shorter bolts or add washers under the bolt heads if there is any doubt. Maximum engagement is approximately two full turns before the bolt exits the flange.

**Probe must not contact tank surfaces.** The bent probe must clear both the tank top and tank bottom with the sender head seated against the SAE-5 flange. Contact will damage the sender and may cause a false reading or mechanical failure.

## Prerequisites

- Fuel tanks empty and purged of fuel vapor.
- Strake baggage compartment accessible.

## Procedure

**Probe shaping:**

1. Identify the bendable portion of the probe arm. Mark the allowable bend zone — do not bend outside this zone.
2. Bend the probe so it curves upward toward the wire side of the sender head, high enough to nearly contact the inner top surface of the tank, then back down so the float tip nearly contacts the inner bottom surface.
3. Dry-fit the sender against the SAE-5 flange with the gasket in place. Verify the probe clears both the tank top and tank bottom with no contact. Adjust the bend and repeat until clearance is confirmed.

**Bolt length check:**

4. Test-fit the mounting bolts with the gasket. Confirm the bolt tips do not protrude through the SAE-5 flange. If they do, substitute shorter bolts or add washers under the bolt heads.

**Installation:**

5. Clean both mating surfaces (sender head and SAE-5 flange) with isopropyl alcohol. Allow to dry.
6. Apply a thin, uniform layer of Permatex Form-A-Gasket No. 2 to one side of the neoprene gasket. Position the gasket on the SAE-5 flange. Apply a second thin layer to the gasket's exposed face.
7. Seat the sender head against the gasket. Install all five bolts with washers finger-tight.
8. Tighten the five bolts evenly in a cross pattern until the gasket just begins to bulge. Do not overtighten — maximum approximately two full turns from finger-tight.

> **NOTE:** Allow 4 hours before fueling. Full cure of the sealant is reached after 24 hours.

**Wiring:**

9. Install a connector on the wire leads from the sender head. @@TOM: verify pin count and wire colors at aircraft.
10. Route and connect the wiring to the EMS per the EMS installation instructions.

**Calibration:**

11. Calibrate the sender at the empty position (tanks empty).
12. Fill the tanks and calibrate the sender at the full position.
13. Calibrate the sender/EMS combination per the Garmin G3X Installation Manual, section 30.4.32.12.1, Fuel Quantity Calibration.

## Post-Procedure Checks

- Verify no fuel leaks at the mounting flange after fueling.
- Verify the EMS displays a fuel quantity reading consistent with known fuel state for each tank.
