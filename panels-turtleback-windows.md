## Description

The turtleback windows are two tinted acrylic panels embedded in the turtleback structure on each side of the fuselage. They provide lateral visibility for the rear seat passengers. Each window measures approximately 18 in. wide × 14 in. tall.

## Inspection

Inspect the turtleback windows at each condition inspection.

**Acrylic surface:**

- **Crazing**: Look for a network of fine surface cracks. Crazing results from stress, solvent contact, or heat. Any crazing warrants evaluation for replacement. (AC 43.13, ch03_p020)
- **Cracks**: At the first sign of crack development, stop-drill the crack ends with a #30 (1/8 in.) drill to prevent further propagation. Schedule for repair or replacement. (AC 43.13, ch03_p024, ch03_p040)
- **Scratches**: Deep scratches that impair visibility require repair or replacement. Minor surface scratches may be polished out per the procedure in AC 43.13, ch03_p040.
- **Optical clarity**: Check for hazing, yellowing, or optical distortion.

> **NOTE:** Replace rather than repair extensively damaged transparent plastic whenever possible. Repairs must not be located in the pilot's line of vision. (AC 43.13, ch03_p024)

**Structure:**

- Inspect the resin/flox bond around the perimeter of each window. Each window is set into a slot routed approximately 1/2 in. into the fiberglass-foam-fiberglass turtleback skin and bonded in place with resin and cotton flox. Check for cracks, gaps, or separation between the window edge and the surrounding flox.
