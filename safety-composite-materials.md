The airframe of N657CZ is fiberglass/epoxy composite construction. Routine inspection, surface preparation, and repair work all involve contact with composite materials. Hazards differ depending on whether the material is cured or uncured and what work is being performed.

## Fiberglass Dust

Sanding, grinding, drilling, or cutting fiberglass generates fine glass fiber dust that causes skin and respiratory irritation (AC 43.13, ch03_p002).

**PPE required:**
- Half-face respirator with P100 particulate filters, or N95 minimum
- Nitrile gloves
- Safety glasses or goggles
- Long sleeves

Work in a well-ventilated area or outdoors. Sanding generates static charges that attract dust and contamination to the work surface — wipe surfaces down after sanding before applying resin (AC 43.13, ch03_p002).

## Uncured Epoxy

Epoxy resin and hardener are hazardous in liquid form. Cured epoxy is inert — the hazard is from handling uncured material during mixing and application.

**Skin sensitization** is the primary long-term risk. Epoxy is a known sensitizer: repeated skin contact — even in small amounts — can cause permanent sensitization. Once sensitized, any subsequent epoxy exposure triggers an allergic reaction. Sensitization is irreversible.

**Amine hardener off-gassing** causes eye and mucous membrane irritation. Ventilate the work area whenever mixing or applying epoxy (AC 43.13, ch01_p020).

**PPE required:**
- Nitrile gloves — latex is inadequate; use nitrile or neoprene
- Safety glasses or goggles
- Adequate shop ventilation

Review the Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS) for the specific epoxy system before starting any repair. Consult the MSDS for first aid, handling precautions, and disposal requirements (AC 43.13, ch03_p001; ch01_p018).

## General Precautions

- Federal law requires chemical manufacturers to provide MSDS with health hazard data, first aid information, and handling precautions (AC 43.13, ch01_p018). Obtain and read the MSDS before working with any unfamiliar product.
- Do not use chemical paint strippers on fiberglass laminates (AC 43.13, ch03_p002).
- Keep the shop clean. Contaminated work surfaces and tools spread uncured resin and hardener (AC 43.13, ch01_p020).
- Dispose of mixed epoxy, used gloves, and saturated rags per local regulations. Exothermic reaction in improperly disposed mixed epoxy can cause fire.
