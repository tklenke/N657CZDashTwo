# Canopy Cleaning

[TOC]

## Overview

This procedure covers routine cleaning and polishing of the canopy. Clean the canopy whenever surface contamination is present and after any flight that deposits insects, oil, or exhaust residue on the surface.

> **NOTE:** This procedure also applies to the turtleback windows. See [[Turtleback Windows|panels-turtleback-windows]].

## Tools and Materials Required

- Water source (hose or bucket)
- Clean cotton or microfiber cloths (minimum two)
- Plexus Aviation Plastic Cleaner and Polish
- Pledge furniture wax (optional)

## Safety Notes

**Never use paper towels.** Paper towels scratch acrylic. Use only clean cotton or microfiber cloths.

**Never wipe a dry or dusty canopy.** Wiping before rinsing grinds abrasive particles into the surface. Always flush with water first.

**Never use the following — they soften acrylic and cause crazing:** (AC 43.13, ch03_p025)

- Gasoline
- Alcohol
- Benzene
- Acetone
- Carbon tetrachloride
- Fire extinguisher fluids
- De-icing fluids
- Lacquer thinners
- Window cleaning sprays (including Windex and similar household products)

## Prerequisites

None. Canopy may be cleaned open or closed.

## Procedure

1. Pour water over the entire canopy surface to flush loose dust and dirt. Do not wipe yet.
2. Apply Plexus to a clean cotton or microfiber cloth. Do not spray Plexus directly onto the canopy.
3. Wipe the canopy with light, overlapping strokes. Avoid heavy pressure.
4. Buff to optical clarity with a second clean, dry cloth.
5. *(Optional)* Apply a thin, even coat of Pledge furniture wax. Buff to a high polish with a clean cloth.

## Post-Procedure Checks

- Verify no streaking, hazing, or cleaning residue remains on the canopy surface.
- Verify no Plexus or wax residue has accumulated in the canopy seal or latch mechanism.
