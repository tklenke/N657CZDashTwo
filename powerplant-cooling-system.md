## Description

The cooling system regulates oil temperature using an oil cooler mounted behind the strake inside the engine cowling. Airflow through the oil cooler is controlled by a cockpit-operated louver assembly.

The NACA-style flush inlet (National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics) on the belly pressurizes the cowling interior. Cooling air enters the oil cooler from the top through the louver inlet and exits out the bottom of the lower cowl. The louvers regulate oil temperature by restricting or opening the inlet — not the outlet.

## Operation

The louver control adjusts oil temperature during flight. Open louvers allow maximum airflow through the oil cooler; closed louvers restrict airflow to retain heat.

- **Cold weather:** Close louvers partially or fully to prevent overcooling.
- **Hot weather:** Open louvers fully.

## Inspection

Inspect the cooling system at each condition inspection.

**Oil cooler:**
- Inspect for physical damage, dents, or deformation.
- Check all fittings and connections for leaks or seepage.
- Inspect mounting for security and condition.

**Louver assembly:**
- Inspect louver vanes for damage, bending, or wear.
- Check rivets, screws, and mounting hardware for looseness or corrosion.
- Verify sealing material (foam or gasket) is intact and provides closure when louvers are shut.
- Verify the tension spring is present and functional — louvers must return to open position when cable tension is released.

**Control cable:**
- Inspect for fraying, kinking, or corrosion along the full cable run.
- Check cable ends and attach points for security.
- Verify cable moves freely without binding.

**Functional test:**
- Operate the louver control through full travel. Louvers must open and close fully without binding.
- Verify louvers return to open position when cockpit control is released (spring action).
- If you notice resistance, investigate before flight — do not force the mechanism.
